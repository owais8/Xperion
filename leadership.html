How we lead

Six principles that shape how this team operates.

The shorthand for what leadership at Xperion looks like, how decisions get made, how people get treated, how we choose what to ship.

The senior partner is on the work.

The named partner on your engagement is the person doing or directly overseeing the work, not a relationship manager performing it via someone junior.

Disagreement is part of the job.

Leaders are expected to push back, on the brief, on the scope, on each other. The work is better for an honest disagreement than a polite agreement.

Compounding people, not extracting them.

We invest in long careers, not staffing utilisation. Leaders are accountable for whether their people are growing, not just whether they're billable.

Outcomes over titles.

Titles describe accountability, not status. We expect leaders to do the work where the work needs them, including the unglamorous parts that title alone could excuse.

Honest with clients, even when it costs revenue.

If we are not the right partner for a project, we say so. If a client is making a decision we think will hurt the outcome, we say so.

The flywheel beats the silo.

Practice directors are accountable for the integrated flywheel, not just their practice's numbers. Cross-practice context is the unfair advantage.
