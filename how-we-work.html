Why this model

Predictability is the most expensive feature of professional services.

Most agencies and consultancies sell creativity, expertise, and access. They under-sell the operating model, and the operating model is where engagements quietly succeed or fail.

We treat the operating model as a deliverable. The same four-phase rhythm, the same weekly cadence, the same review artefacts, predictable enough that you can plan around us, and rigorous enough that the work compounds.

Operating models are boring on purpose. Boring is what makes the work calm enough to be excellent.
  • ▲
    One model across five practicesWhether we're staffing, building, advising, marketing, or designing, the cadence, gates, and review artefacts are consistent.
  • ⊕
    Named accountabilityEvery engagement has one named partner. You always know whose phone rings when something needs a course correction.
  • ✓
    Decision documents at every gatePhase transitions ship with a signed decision document. We do not let scope drift quietly into the next phase.
  • ◆
    Transparent by defaultYou see what we see, tasks, decisions, blockers, in your tools, in real time. No surprise status decks.
Four phases

Discovery → Definition → Delivery → Optimise.

Every engagement runs the same four phases, with measurable gates between each. The phases scale from a two-week sprint to a multi-quarter programme.

Phase 01 · 1–2 wks
▲

Discovery, Align & scope

Stakeholder alignment, technical or domain scoping, and success-metric definition. We invest in understanding the business before proposing anything.

  • Stakeholder interviews & baseline review
  • Success-metric & outcome definition
  • Constraints, dependencies, and risks
  • Costed proposal within 48 hours of close
Phase 02 · 1–4 wks
◈

Definition, Plan with gates

Validated architecture, role definition, or messaging architecture, depending on practice. Sequenced plan with named decision gates.

  • Architecture, design, or operating-model artefacts
  • Sequenced plan with named decision gates
  • Risk register and dependency map
  • Signed decision document at phase close
Phase 03 · Ongoing
⊞

Delivery, Iterate & ship

Agile delivery with continuous visibility. Sprint cadence, weekly demos, monthly steering reviews. Whatever the practice, the rhythm is consistent.

  • Weekly demos & reviews
  • Sprint visibility in your tools
  • Open issue tracker, open decisions
  • Monthly steering & QBR cadence
Phase 04 · Ongoing
↺

Optimise, Compound the wins

Post-launch optimisation, team scaling, performance tuning, and the second-order improvements that compound. Engagements stay productive across years.

  • Performance tuning & cost optimisation
  • Team scaling or transition
  • Quarterly business reviews
  • Roadmap rebaselining

Communications

You see what we see.

Communications, decisions, and artefacts live in your tools, in real time. No parallel vendor workflows, no surprise status decks, no information asymmetry.

In your tools, not ours.

We work in your Slack, your Jira, your GitHub, your Figma, your CRM. Specialists embed into your workflows rather than running parallel ones.

Decisions captured, dated, signed.

Every significant decision lands as an ADR or decision document, with the context that drove it. Future trade-offs land with context rather than re-litigation.

Risks raised early, not late.

If we see a delivery, scope, or outcome risk forming, we raise it the day we see it, not the day it lands. The engagement manager owns this discipline.

Bad news in person.

Good news travels in writing. Bad news travels in a call. We do not let difficult conversations land first in a deck or an email.

FAQ

Questions clients ask before signing.

Do we sign one MSA or a separate contract per practice?

One MSA. Add new practices, new roles, or new scopes under the existing agreement via change orders. We exist specifically to eliminate the procurement overhead of managing separate vendors.

How do you handle confidentiality and IP?

Mutual NDA, work-for-hire IP assignment, and your security review pack covered by default. We can sign your paper, or our paper, or a redlined hybrid, whatever closes faster.

What if a specialist or build doesn't work out?

30-day satisfaction guarantee. If a specialist isn't a fit in the first 30 days, we replace at no additional cost. For build engagements, we have hyper-care and a structured course-correction protocol that catches issues in week one or two.

Can we end the engagement early?

Yes. Our standard contracts have 30-day notice for termination on either side. We do not believe in lock-ins that hide poor performance.

Do you work fixed-price or T&M?

Both, depending on the practice. Strategy sprints and AI pilots are typically fixed-price with a defined deliverable. Augmentation and build programmes are typically monthly retainers or T&M with sprint-aligned billing.

Are your specialists employees or contractors?

Most are W-2 employees of Xperion or our regional entities; a small number are long-tenured contractors. Either way, they are subject to the same vetting, engagement management, and accountability.

Where do you operate?

Xperion engagements span North America, EMEA, and APAC. Our delivery teams operate in the time zones our clients need to work in.

How quickly can we start?

From signed brief to embedded specialist: 1–2 weeks. From signed brief to AI pilot kickoff: typically inside a week. Faster than your in-house hiring cycle, in every case.

Ready to test the operating model?

One discovery call. A 48-hour proposal at the close. You'll see exactly what an engagement with us looks like before you sign anything, phases, cadence, deliverables, and the named partner.
